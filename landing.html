Yeah, But...

"I already have TradingView. Can't I just use screeners?" ⌄

TradingView screeners filter on indicators. None of them understand candle formation — inside vs expanding, AMDX vs XAMD. None of them read whether a manipulation candle agreed or disagreed with a liquidity draw. You'd need custom Pine scripts for each timeframe, and you'd still manually cross-reference. That's exactly the 2-hour problem we solve.

"Is this a signal service?" ⌄

No. We don't tell you what to buy or sell. We classify candle formation and detect draw on liquidity — the scanner gives you the model context. You still read the price. You still apply your setup — breakers, order blocks, OTE, whatever your edge is. This is a research tool, not a guru.

"How accurate is the A/X classification?" ⌄

We use extended hours data (4am-8pm ET with volume filtering) to match what you see on TradingView's 24H candles. Plus a 0.1% tolerance for data feed discrepancies. The result: candle formations match what you'd mark by hand on TradingView — we just do it for 200+ tickers in under a minute.

"What if I only trade 20 tickers?" ⌄

Then you're probably missing setups. The best A candle with swept SSL and a disagreed manipulation — the signature reversal — might be on a ticker you've never looked at. Cast a wide net, then filter down to your criteria. Every candle is only known by the prior candle. More candles = more edge.

"I'm not profitable yet. Will this help?" ⌄

This won't make a losing strategy profitable. It won't teach you to read price. But if you already understand candle formation, agree/disagree, draw on liquidity, and how sweeps set up reversals — this removes the bottleneck between knowing how to read price and finding what to read. The analysis is still yours.
